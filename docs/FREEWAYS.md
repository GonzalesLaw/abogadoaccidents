## I-10 Freeway — San Bernardino County Corridor

**Full name:** Christopher Columbus Transcontinental Highway
**Route through IE:** Los Angeles County line → Pomona → Ontario → Montclair → Upland → Rancho Cucamonga → Fontana → Rialto → Colton → San Bernardino → Redlands → Yucaipa → Beaumont → Banning → Cabazon → Palm Springs
**Daily volume:** ~200,000 vehicles
**Speed limit:** 65 mph

### Why I-10 Is So Dangerous
The I-10 through the Inland Empire is one of the busiest commercial freight corridors in the United States. Billions of dollars of goods move through this corridor daily between the ports of Los Angeles/Long Beach and the rest of the country. This means 24/7 commercial truck traffic mixing with passenger commuters at 65+ mph.

### Most Dangerous Segments
- **I-10/I-15 interchange (Fontana):** One of the highest-accident interchanges in California due to complex merging, high speed, and heavy volumes from both directions
- **Cherry Ave on/off ramps (Fontana):** Frequent rear-end crashes during congestion
- **Tippecanoe Ave interchange (San Bernardino):** High merge-related accident volume
- **San Gorgonio Pass (Beaumont/Banning):** Extreme wind conditions regularly overturn high-profile vehicles and blow debris across lanes
- **Redlands Blvd interchange:** High weaving crash frequency

### Common Case Types on I-10
- Semi-truck rear-end collisions in slow traffic
- Chain-reaction pileups during fog, rain, or wind events
- Debris and blown tire accidents from commercial vehicles
- Wrong-way DUI crashes (typically late night/early morning)
- Merging collisions at the I-15 interchange

## I-15 Freeway — Cajon Pass to Temecula

**Route through IE:** Cajon Pass → Hesperia → Victorville (High Desert spur) → Fontana → Ontario → Norco → Corona → Lake Elsinore → Murrieta → Temecula → San Diego County
**Daily volume:** ~150,000 vehicles
**Speed limit:** 65–70 mph

### Why I-15 Is So Dangerous
The I-15 is the primary route between Los Angeles/Inland Empire and Las Vegas, creating massive vacation/weekend traffic spikes. It also carries enormous freight volume. The Cajon Pass section is arguably the most dangerous stretch of highway in the entire Inland Empire.

### Cajon Pass — Most Dangerous Grade
- **Elevation:** 4,190 feet at summit
- **Grade:** 6% downhill toward San Bernardino
- **Length:** Approximately 8 miles of steep descent
- **Hazards:** Truck brake failures, tire blowouts, overturned vehicles, ice and snow in winter, dense fog, high winds (Santa Ana conditions)
- **Fatal accidents:** Multiple per year — trucking companies know the risk and are represented by insurance defense teams immediately

### Most Dangerous I-15 Segments
- **Cajon Pass (I-15/SR-138 junction):** Truck runaway incidents, wrong-way crashes
- **I-15/I-215 "The Y" (Murrieta):** One of Southern California's most complex and crash-prone interchanges
- **I-15/I-10 interchange (Ontario/Rancho Cucamonga):** High merge-conflict crash volume
- **Corona/Norco segment:** Stop-and-go commuter congestion causing rear-ends

## SR-91 Freeway — Riverside Freeway

**Route through IE:** Orange County line → Corona → Riverside (terminates at I-215)
**Daily volume:** 300,000+ vehicles at peak
**Speed limit:** 65 mph
**Express Lanes:** Yes — 91 Express Lanes (toll)

### Why SR-91 Is California's Most Dangerous Commute
The SR-91 corridor is consistently ranked among the top 5 most congested freeways in the United States. Stop-and-go traffic for 20–40 miles creates perfect rear-end chain-crash conditions. The 91 was built in the 1960s and was never designed for current traffic volumes.

### Most Dangerous SR-91 Segments
- **SR-91/I-15 interchange (Corona):** Among the highest-volume highway-to-highway merges in SoCal — massive weaving zone
- **91 Express Lanes merge/diverge points:** Drivers entering/exiting the toll lanes at high speed create sudden lane changes
- **SR-91 westbound PM peak (3–7 PM):** Backed up from Riverside to Orange County — rear-end incidents daily
- **SR-91/SR-71 interchange:** Multiple ramp merges in tight space

### Common Cases on SR-91
- Rear-end collisions in stop-and-go traffic
- Express Lane merge crashes
- Aggressive driving and road rage incidents
- Multi-vehicle pileups

## SR-60 Freeway — Moreno Valley Freeway

**Route through IE:** Los Angeles County line → Pomona → Ontario → Jurupa Valley → Riverside → Moreno Valley (terminates at I-215)
**Daily volume:** ~120,000 vehicles
**Speed limit:** 65 mph

### Why SR-60 Is the IE's Deadliest Truck Road
The SR-60 corridor bisects the largest concentration of warehouses and distribution centers in North America — the Inland Empire logistics hub. Amazon, Walmart, IKEA, and hundreds of other companies operate facilities along this corridor. The result: 24/7 commercial truck traffic on every ramp, interchange, and surface street intersection.

### Most Dangerous SR-60 Segments
- **SR-60/I-215 interchange (Riverside):** One of the busiest interchange points in Riverside County — high weave and merge crash volume
- **SR-60/I-15 interchange (Ontario/Jurupa Valley border):** Heavy truck diverge zone
- **Etiwanda Ave interchange:** High-volume industrial area with constant truck in/out
- **Moreno Valley corridor:** Multiple off-ramp crashes near the Moreno Valley Mall area

### Common Cases on SR-60
- Truck vs. passenger car collisions (often catastrophic)
- Cargo spill accidents creating debris fields
- Rear-end crashes in construction zones
- Side-swipe accidents from trucks changing lanes

## I-215 Freeway — San Bernardino to Murrieta

**Route through IE:** San Bernardino → Riverside → Perris → Menifee → Murrieta (merges into I-15 at "The Y")
**Daily volume:** ~100,000+ vehicles
**Speed limit:** 65 mph

### I-215 Growth Corridor Dangers
The I-215 is the fastest-growing corridor in the Inland Empire — new residential and commercial development is exploding between Perris and Menifee, but road infrastructure is not keeping pace. Daily rush hour backups and dangerous merging situations at underfunded interchange points are endemic.

### Most Dangerous I-215 Segments
- **I-215/SR-60 interchange (Riverside):** High-volume, complex interchange
- **Perris Blvd area:** Rapid residential growth with inadequate ramp spacing
- **I-215/I-15 "The Y" (Murrieta):** See I-15 section above
- **Ethanac Rd ramps (Menifee):** New development creating high-volume on/off movement

## SR-138 — Cajon Pass Connector

**Route:** I-15 (Cajon Pass) → Victorville → Palmdale (SR-138 also runs through the Antelope Valley)
**Key risk:** The SR-138/I-15 junction at the top of Cajon Pass is where truck runaway accidents and wrong-way crashes frequently originate. Steep grades, switchbacks, and wind conditions make this one of California's most dangerous non-freeway mountain roads.

## SR-74 — Ortega Highway

**Route:** Lake Elsinore → Cleveland National Forest → Orange County
**Why it's deadly:** The Ortega Highway is a two-lane mountain road with sharp curves, limited guardrails, sheer drop-offs, and a 55 mph speed limit that drivers routinely exceed. Fatal head-on and single-vehicle accidents occur multiple times per year. Gonzales Law Offices handles Ortega Highway accident cases throughout Riverside County.

## SR-38 — Big Bear Highway

**Route:** Redlands → Yucaipa → Big Bear Lake
**Why it's dangerous:** SR-38 ascends through the San Bernardino Mountains with narrow lanes, limited shoulders, ice and snow in winter, and significant recreational traffic (hikers, cyclists, motorcyclists). Speed differential between passenger vehicles and recreational trucks creates dangerous passing situations.
