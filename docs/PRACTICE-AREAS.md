## Car Accident Law

### Rear-End Collisions
Rear-end accidents are the most common type of car crash in California. The rear driver is generally presumed at fault, but comparative fault arguments, brake-checking claims, and road condition factors can complicate liability. Gonzales Law Offices handles thousands of rear-end cases from minor whiplash injuries to catastrophic spinal cord damage.

**Common locations in the Inland Empire:** I-10 near Cherry Ave (Fontana), Sierra Ave intersections, Foothill Blvd (Rancho Cucamonga), SR-91 (Corona), Alessandro Blvd (Moreno Valley)

### T-Bone (Side-Impact) Collisions
Side-impact crashes at intersections are among the most deadly — the side of a vehicle offers far less protection than the front or rear. T-bone collisions frequently result in TBI, rib fractures, pelvic injuries, and internal organ damage. Red-light violations and failure-to-yield are the leading causes.

### Head-On Collisions
Head-on crashes are among the most catastrophic, often resulting in fatalities or permanent disability. Common causes include wrong-way drivers (often DUI-related), overtaking on two-lane roads, and distracted driving. Gonzales Law Offices pursues the full value of these life-altering cases including punitive damages in DUI cases.

### Hit & Run Accidents
California law requires drivers to stop after an accident. When the at-fault driver flees, victims can pursue compensation through their own Uninsured Motorist coverage. Time is critical — surveillance footage must be preserved and witnesses contacted immediately. Call Gonzales Law Offices immediately after a hit-and-run.

### Multi-Vehicle Pileups
Chain-reaction accidents on California freeways involve complex multi-party liability. Gonzales Law Offices investigates all contributing parties — drivers, trucking companies, road maintenance agencies — to build the maximum recovery from all available insurance sources.

## Truck & Commercial Vehicle Accidents

### Semi-Truck Accidents
Commercial trucking accidents are among the most complex and highest-value personal injury cases. Federal FMCSA regulations govern every aspect of commercial trucking — hours of service, drug testing, maintenance logs, weight limits, and cargo securement. Violations of FMCSA rules can establish negligence per se.

**Key evidence to preserve immediately:**
- Electronic Logging Device (ELD) / black box data
- Dash cam and exterior camera footage
- Driver logbooks and records of duty status
- Drug and alcohol testing records
- Truck maintenance and inspection records
- Cargo manifests and weight tickets

Gonzales Law Offices issues immediate legal preservation demands to prevent destruction of this time-sensitive evidence.

### Delivery Truck Accidents
Amazon, UPS, FedEx, and DHL drivers operate under extreme time pressure, leading to unsafe driving behavior. Companies often classify drivers as independent contractors to shield themselves from liability — but California law provides strong protections for accident victims. Gonzales Law Offices pierces these corporate structures to identify all liable parties.

### Bus Accidents
Public bus accidents (Omnitrans, RTA, SBCTA) involve government entities and special claims procedures. Private bus accidents may involve motor carrier insurance. Claims against government entities have a strict 6-month government tort claim deadline.

## Motorcycle Accidents

Motorcyclists are extremely vulnerable to severe injury — no metal frame, airbags, or seat belts protect them in a crash. Insurance companies routinely attempt to bias against motorcyclists by arguing they were speeding or lane-splitting unsafely. Gonzales Law Offices builds powerful evidence packages — including accident reconstruction experts and motorcycle safety specialists — to counter these tactics.

**Common injuries:** Road rash, traumatic brain injury, spinal fractures, broken bones, internal bleeding, PTSD

**California lane-splitting:** Legal in California under Assembly Bill 51. Lane-splitting does not automatically make the motorcyclist at fault.

## Pedestrian Accidents

Pedestrians have virtually no protection in a vehicle collision. Even low-speed impacts cause serious injuries. California law gives pedestrians the right of way in marked and unmarked crosswalks, though contributory negligence can be argued. Common causes: distracted driving, failure to yield, running red lights, DUI.

**High-risk areas:** Foothill Blvd crosswalks, Sierra Ave near schools, bus stop areas throughout San Bernardino and Fontana.

## Bicycle Accidents

Cyclists can sue for all damages suffered in accidents caused by vehicle drivers, road defect negligence (city/county liability), or defective equipment (product liability). California's "3-foot law" requires drivers to give cyclists at least 3 feet of clearance when passing. Violations create strong liability arguments.

## Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

TBI is caused by sudden trauma to the head — common in car accidents from head-to-steering-wheel impact, airbag deployment, or violent acceleration/deceleration (coup-contrecoup). Symptoms range from headache and dizziness to permanent cognitive impairment, personality changes, and inability to work.

**Diagnosis tools:** CT scan, MRI, neuropsychological testing, SPECT scan

**Settlement range:** Six to seven figures for cases with documented lasting impairment. Gonzales Law Offices partners with board-certified neurologists and life-care planners to build maximum-value TBI cases.

## Spinal Cord Injuries

Spinal injuries are among the most expensive and life-altering injuries possible. Cases may involve herniated discs (L4-L5, L5-S1, C5-C6, C6-C7 are most common), spinal cord compression, paralysis (paraplegia or tetraplegia), and chronic nerve pain. Treatment costs for severe spinal cord injuries can exceed $1 million over a lifetime.

**Common treatments:** Physical therapy, epidural steroid injections, radiofrequency ablation, discectomy, spinal fusion surgery, disc replacement surgery

Gonzales Law Offices works with neurosurgeons and life-care planners to quantify the full lifetime cost of spinal injuries before any settlement is accepted.

## Wrongful Death

When a loved one is killed due to another driver's negligence, surviving family members may pursue a wrongful death lawsuit in California. Eligible plaintiffs include spouses, domestic partners, children, parents, and financial dependents.

**Recoverable damages:**
- Funeral and burial expenses
- Lost financial support (lifetime earnings the deceased would have provided)
- Loss of companionship, comfort, and consortium
- Loss of household services
- Punitive damages for gross negligence or DUI

The estate may separately file a **survival action** for the deceased person's own pain and suffering, medical expenses, and lost earnings from the accident to the time of death.

**Deadline:** 2 years from the date of death.

Gonzales Law Offices handles wrongful death cases with both the compassion the family deserves and the aggressive legal strategy the case demands.

## Uninsured & Underinsured Motorist Claims

California requires all drivers to carry minimum liability insurance, but approximately 15–17% of California drivers are uninsured. Your own Uninsured Motorist (UM) coverage compensates you when the at-fault driver has no insurance. Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage applies when the at-fault driver's policy limits are insufficient to cover your damages.

Gonzales Law Offices pursues UM/UIM claims aggressively — your own insurer has the same duty to compensate you fairly, and Gonzales Law Offices holds them to that duty.

## Insurance Bad Faith

California's Insurance Code and common law impose a duty of good faith and fair dealing on all insurance companies. Bad faith occurs when an insurer:
- Unreasonably denies a valid claim
- Unreasonably delays payment
- Makes a settlement offer far below the clear value of the claim
- Fails to investigate the claim promptly
- Misrepresents policy terms or coverage

Victims of insurance bad faith can recover not only their policy benefits but also consequential damages and punitive damages. Gonzales Law Offices pursues bad faith claims against both the at-fault driver's insurer and the victim's own insurer when they act in bad faith.

## Slip & Fall (Premises Liability)

Property owners have a duty to maintain safe conditions. Slip and fall accidents caused by wet floors, uneven pavement, poor lighting, or inadequate warnings may create liability for property owners, businesses, or government entities. Cases against government entities require a 6-month government tort claim filing.
